The Gap

Training infrastructure in India is world-class. CrossFit boxes, F45 studios, boutique fitness — all growing at 18.8% CAGR. Yet recovery is treated as an afterthought. No cold plunge. No contrast therapy. No guided protocol. No community. The work breaks you down. Recovery builds you up. Only one side of that equation currently exists.

The Human Cost

Week three feels harder than week one. Tightness becomes stiffness. Fatigue becomes injury. Consistency becomes burnout. The serious athlete — marathon runner, CrossFitter, longevity-focused tech professional — has no dedicated space to restore. They train in premium environments. They recover nowhere.

Why These Markets — India First Strategy

Hyderabad offers a first-mover advantage — lower setup costs, a growing fitness base anchored by communities like Hyderabad Runners Society, and a tech-driven population already primed for recovery. From there, we scale into Mumbai for premium monetization and brand visibility, and Bangalore for its performance-focused, early-adopter audience — together capturing India's full spectrum of fitness demand, from emerging adoption to serious recovery spend. Globally, the category is being defined by Othership, Rebase Recovery, Bathhouse, and Remedy Place. No scaled equivalent exists in India today.

The Customer Profile

Age 25–45, upper-middle to HNI. Already spending ₹8,000–18,000 on individual IV sessions. Already buying NormaTec home devices at ₹84,999. Marathon runners, CrossFitters, and tech professionals following Huberman protocols. The customer is pre-educated. They're waiting for the facility.
